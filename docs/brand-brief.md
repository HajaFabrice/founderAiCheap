# Brand Brief

This brief governs the public OPLURIX site, product pages, campaign pages, social visuals, lead magnets, and customer-facing copy.

## Brand

OPLURIX means ecological intelligence made useful.

It is not a generic AI brand. It is a field-grounded conservation platform that turns real ecological work, messy observations, biodiversity data, and hard-won field lessons into products, public communication, services, and research visibility.

Core line:

> Ecological Intelligence for Conservation

Working promise:

> Turn field reality into clear, credible tools people can use.

## What OPLURIX Exists To Do

OPLURIX exists because valuable conservation knowledge often stays trapped in notebooks, internal reports, field memories, unshared datasets, and underfunded projects.

The platform helps that knowledge move into:

- credible public communication
- practical digital products
- funder-ready narratives
- reproducible workflows
- field-aware AI systems
- visible research campaigns

## Audience

Primary audiences:

- Conservation professionals who have real field experience but struggle to communicate it publicly.
- Field biologists, graduate researchers, and NGO staff who need practical systems, not abstract theory.
- Biodiversity teams who need better data, visibility, reporting, training, or donor communication.
- Mission-aligned supporters who want to understand and support Madagascar-based ecological research.

Secondary audiences:

- Funders, collaborators, and institutions evaluating the seriousness of the work.
- Technical contributors helping improve the repo-based platform.
- Buyers who need small, useful, low-risk products before they trust larger offers.

## Brand Personality

OPLURIX should feel:

- Field-grounded.
- Intelligent.
- Calmly ambitious.
- Useful.
- Honest about limits.
- Protective of scientific integrity.
- Warm without becoming soft.

It should not feel:

- Like a generic AI hustle.
- Like a growth-hacking brand with green decoration.
- Like a luxury agency pretending to be conservation.
- Like academic prose without a next step.
- Like a grant proposal trying to impress everyone at once.

## Voice

Use second person when speaking to the visitor.

Use active, short, concrete sentences.

Lead with the visitor's real friction:

- You have field notes but no publishing system.
- You have useful data but no clean story.
- You have conservation work that deserves more visibility.
- You need practical tools without pretending AI replaces judgment.

Then connect the work to OPLURIX.

Preferred tone:

- direct
- grounded
- precise
- encouraging
- evidence-aware
- professionally warm

Avoid:

- hype
- fake urgency
- inflated claims
- vague impact language
- "revolutionary" unless the evidence truly supports it
- claims that a product guarantees publication, funding, influence, or conservation outcomes

### Typography

Current public-site families:

- `IBM Plex Sans` for body copy.
- `IBM Plex Mono` for labels, pills, metadata, nav, and operational signals.
- `Playfair Display` for editorial headings where the page needs warmth and authority.

Do not replace these casually. If a new page needs a different typographic system, document the reason.

### Logo

Use `docs/assets/oplurix-logo.png` for compact brand marks.

Use the branded forest/drone cover assets for high-impact hero or share-preview moments, not as repeated decoration.

Logo rules:

- Keep enough padding around the lizard/optics mark.
- Do not place the logo on busy backgrounds without a cream or dark containment shape.
- Do not stretch or recolor the mark unless creating a deliberate new asset.
- Avoid using the logo as a substitute for a clear headline.

## Visual Language

OPLURIX visuals should combine:

- Madagascar field ecology
- forest texture
- thermal/drone sensing
- research coordinates
- subtle topographic or grid overlays
- gold-highlighted ecological signals
- calm scientific authority

Preferred imagery:

- forests seen from above
- thermal or detection overlays
- field notebooks, transects, maps, survey equipment
- lemurs and biodiversity only when relevant and respectful
- conservation work in context, not stock "green impact" symbolism

Avoid:

- generic corporate leaves
- fake futuristic neon dashboards
- overused AI robot imagery
- biodiversity images that imply work not actually done
- visuals that make Madagascar look like a vague exotic backdrop

## Copy Architecture

Most public pages should follow this order:

1. Who this is for.
2. The real problem or friction.
3. The immediate useful next step.
4. Proof or field context.
5. The broader OPLURIX mission.
6. One primary CTA and one secondary CTA.

For cold traffic:

- Lead with the checklist or a clear educational entry point.

For warm traffic:

- Lead with Product 1.

For hot traffic:

- Send to direct Payhip checkout.

For research-support traffic:

- Send to the ATBC research campaign page.

## CTA Language

Preferred CTA verbs:

- Get the checklist
- Open the live product
- See what is inside
- Support the ATBC trip
- Join OPLURIX updates
- Request contact
- Read the research context

Avoid vague CTAs:

- Learn more
- Discover
- Click here
- Start your journey

Use "live product" only for Product 1 unless another product has verified checkout and delivery.

## Product Truth Rules

Current truth:

- Product 1 is live: Expert-to-Influencer Content Engine.
- Product 1 uses Payhip checkout today.
- Product 1 has English and French delivery packages.
- Products 2 and 4 have prepared or partially prepared delivery paths and French variants, but checkout should stay off until activation gates are complete.
- Product 3 is visible as a product lane, but should stay held/source-only until equipment specs, market pricing, and the update workflow are refreshed.
- Products 5 to 10 are earlier-stage/source-only unless founder approval changes their state.
- Shopify may be used temporarily as a marketing platform while the repo site remains the authority layer.
- Netlify/GitHub Pages style hosting is the intended lightweight long-term public-site path.

Do not imply:

- instant automated delivery unless tested
- active checkout for Products 2 to 10
- email automation through Kit until it is connected
- support capacity that is not operational
- institutional readiness for source-only products

## Bilingual Rules

English and French pages do not need to be literal translations. They need to be strategically equivalent.

French copy should sound like a real founder speaking to conservation and research professionals, not like translated marketing.

Use French when the audience is Madagascar, francophone Africa, French-speaking researchers, local NGOs, or bilingual partners.

Use English when the audience is international conservation, LinkedIn thought leadership, ATBC, global collaborators, and digital-product buyers.

## Non-Negotiables

- Field reality comes before brand polish.
- Scientific honesty comes before conversion.
- Survival revenue matters, but not at the cost of false claims.
- AI supports judgment; it does not replace ecological responsibility.
- The public site must make the work visible without exaggerating its maturity.
