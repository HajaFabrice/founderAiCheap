Publishing takes years. The reason matters.

The Zonosaurus fieldwork: March to May 2017.
The manuscript status: under review at the African Journal of Herpetology.

The reason for the long timeline is in the paper itself, the discussion section concludes that the low number of observed individuals limits the reliability of potential conclusions, and that the study should be reproduced for several successive years before stronger claims can be made.

That is not a failure of the research. It is the research being honest about its own limits.

And it means that nine years of methodological thinking, field experience, and conceptual development sit between the raw observations and whatever conclusions peer review will eventually accept.

That thinking is not unpublishable. It is just not a results section.

The observations about how cryptic lizards behave as seasons shift, how field methods reach their detection limits, how individual variation challenges species-level generalisations, all of this can be communicated now. Carefully. Without the numbers. Without the claims.

Sharing process is not self-promotion. It is part of what science owes its field.
