research pages are all trying to solve one central problem:

you need a way to move from field truth to public usefulness without becoming
generic, extractive, or dishonest.

That sentence is not branding.
It is the compression of your whole operating philosophy.

You are not building a fake founder story.

## Chapter 2. The Problem You Are Solving

You are solving several problems at once.

The first problem is personal and practical.
You have already done work worth sharing, but valuable conservation knowledge
does not automatically become readable, discoverable, or fundable.

The second problem is sector-wide.
Field teams, researchers, and conservation professionals often generate useful
knowledge that never escapes reports, drives, inboxes, notebooks, or internal
debriefs.

The third problem is methodological.
Conservation work is full of ambiguity, small samples, inconsistent equipment,
messy field conditions, broken continuity, and hard-won practical insight.
That means the work needs translation systems that respect nuance.
It cannot be reduced to influencer theatrics or black-box AI claims.

The fourth problem is structural.
The world does not yet have enough open, auditable, field-grounded ecological
integrity infrastructure, especially in Madagascar and comparable contexts.
That is the larger gap ERIS is meant to address over time.

So the repository now holds four simultaneous answers.

1. `OPLURIX digital products` turn recurring conservation bottlenecks into
   bounded, useful offers.
2. `Public research pages and campaigns` make the work visible without reducing
   it to hype.
3. `Bounded services and Techni-Drones leverage` create immediate survival and
   practical proof.
4. `ERIS and FounderAI` build the deeper governance and systems layer that the
   present work must eventually feed.

Your problem is never just "get customers."

Your real problem is:

how do you create income, legitimacy, visibility, research momentum, and
governance depth without breaking scientific honesty or mission focus?

That is why this repository contains so many different-looking materials.
They are different surfaces of one answer.

## Chapter 10. Metrics, Thresholds, And Risks

You need numbers because mood lies.

### KPI thresholds

| KPI | Green | Yellow | Red | Action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Inbound first-response speed | under 30 min | 30-120 min | over 120 min | Red triggers Zacchaeus and Founder review |
| Outreach personalization quality | over 90% pass | 75-90% | under 75% | Red triggers prompt review and QA tightening |
| Reply rate | over 5% | 2-5% | under 2% | Red triggers messaging and targeting adjustment |
| QA pass rate | over 90% | 80-90% | under 80% | Red triggers Clare escalation and draft review |
| Weekly review cadence | every week | late once | skipped twice | Red triggers Francis escalation |
| Offline queue backlog | 0-3 items | 4-10 items | over 10 items | Red triggers connectivity and replay review |
| Approval turnaround | under 24h | 24-72h | over 72h | Red means protected work is stalling |

Interpretation rules:

- Green means continue and compound.
- Yellow means adjust a workflow, prompt, or target list.
- Red means stop normalizing the problem and surface it explicitly in review.

## Chapter 17. Decision-Making Frameworks

You need simple frameworks because complexity will otherwise make you either
freeze or rationalize.

### Framework 1. The Truth Test

Ask:

- Is this claim true now?
- Is this offer real now?
- Is this route operational now?
- Can a buyer or partner verify what I am saying?

If not, rewrite or delay.

### Framework 2. The Stewardship Test

Ask:

- Does this move protect the mission?
- Does this move avoid extractive drift?
- Does this move preserve scientific honesty?
- Does this move protect sensitive ecological information where needed?

If not, do not proceed.

### Framework 3. The Leverage Test

Ask:

- Does this create immediate cash or dignity?
- Does this create longer-term credibility or protocol?
- Does this create future ERIS leverage?

The best moves usually do at least two of these at once.

### Framework 4. The Approval Test

Ask:

- Is this an external send?
- Is this publish-sensitive?
- Is this financial?
- Is this destructive or security-sensitive?
- Would this be hard to explain after the fact?

If yes, route through approval.

### Framework 5. The Explainability Test

Ask:

- Can you explain the code?
- Can you explain the prompt?
- Can you explain the deliverable?
- Can you explain the research method?
- Can you explain the buyer journey?

If you cannot explain it, you do not own it yet.
