## 3. ERIS — The Core Infrastructure

ERIS stands for **Ecological Integrity Scoring System**. It is the long-term mission of OPLURIX: a field-verified, open-methodology ecological scoring infrastructure for biodiversity MRV in Madagascar and the Global South.

**Why ERIS matters:** Conservation finance instruments — biodiversity credits, carbon co-benefits, REDD+ reporting — currently lack credible, reproducible, field-verified ecological scores. ERIS fills this gap with auditable scores built from actual transect data, camera traps, drone surveys, and acoustic monitoring. Not from models alone.

### 3.1 ERIS Scoring Indicators (v0.1 Concept)

1. **Species richness** — mammals (transect census, camera traps), herpetofauna (visual encounter surveys), birds and bats (AudioMoth)
2. **Vegetation integrity** — botanical plot data, canopy cover, dead/living tree ratios
3. **Thermal habitat quality** — drone thermal surveys; detects wildlife, habitat structure, microclimate variation
4. **Human pressure index** — distance to settlements, deforestation rate, encroachment evidence
5. **MRV confidence score** — data completeness, protocol adherence, reproducibility index

### 3.2 ERIS Principles (All Immutable)

- **Field-verified:** Every score component derives from actual field data, not modelled estimates alone.
- **Open methodology:** Permanently open-access. Every score is reproducible and auditable. No black-box algorithms.
- **MRV-ready:** Designed for direct integration into conservation finance instruments.
- **Steward-owned:** ERIS converts to steward-ownership (Purpose Trust or equivalent) at Year 5.
- **Mission-locked:** Scores will NEVER be sold to or used by extractive industries, fossil fuel companies, or any entity that profits from biodiversity destruction.
