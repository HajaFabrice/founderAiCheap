## Who Are You And What Do You Do?

Q: Who is behind this work?

A: Haja Fabrice Razafindrabe Maminiaina, an environmental data specialist based
in Antananarivo, Madagascar. His work sits at the intersection of biodiversity
field research, ecological data quality, drone-supported conservation methods,
and long-term MRV infrastructure building.

Q: What is ERIS?

A: ERIS is an ecological integrity scoring and governance concept in
development. In practical terms, it is meant to turn biodiversity and forest
monitoring inputs into a transparent, confidence-tagged decision-support score.
At this stage, it is still in pilot and proof-building mode rather than a fully
deployed standard.

Q: What stage is the work at right now?

A: The current stage is practical proof-building: independent biodiversity data
support, grant development, research progression, and pilot-ready methodology
work. The long arc is ERIS, but the immediate work is disciplined service and
evidence accumulation.

## Data Safety And Trust

Q: How do you handle data safety?

A: Sensitive ecological data should be handled conservatively. Endangered
species location details, confidential project data, and restricted partner
information should not be exposed in AI prompts or shared externally without
explicit approval. Traceability and minimum-necessary disclosure are part of the
design.

Q: Can you guarantee the results?

A: Process quality, documentation, and honesty can be guaranteed more safely
than ecological or institutional outcomes. If the data are thin or uncertain,
the correct move is to say so rather than pretending precision.

Q: How do you use AI in communication?

A: AI is used to draft and organize work, but human review remains mandatory.
External-facing communication is review-ready, not autonomous.

Q: Is ERIS already validated across sites?

A: No. ERIS should be described as a pilot-stage methodology and governance
direction unless the prompt packet provides newer verified evidence.

Q: What makes the ERIS approach different?

A: The design emphasis is on transparency, versioned assumptions, confidence
tagging, and auditability rather than black-box scoring.

Q: Can you already provide a formal ERIS score?

A: Only describe that as a pilot or exploratory output unless the request
contains stronger verified deployment facts.

Q: What can you offer an MRV programme right now?

A: Data-pipeline support, data-quality improvement, scoped methodological notes,
and pilot-oriented thinking are safer current claims than full national-scale
MRV delivery.
