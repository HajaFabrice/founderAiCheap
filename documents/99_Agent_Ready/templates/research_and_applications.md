## Template 1: PhD Supervisor First-Contact Email

Goal: one useful reply, not a full application in email form.

```text
Subject: Enquiry regarding potential PhD opportunities in [research area]

Dear Prof./Dr. [Last Name],

I am [Your Name], a conservation biologist from Madagascar with a Master's in Animal Conservation Biology and field research experience in [topic].

I recently read your work on [specific paper or project], especially [one precise point]. It connects closely with my work on [your relevant topic].

I am exploring PhD opportunities in [specific area] and wanted to ask whether you expect doctoral openings or aligned projects in the near future. My background includes [two or three relevant methods or projects].

If useful, I would be glad to share a short research note and CV.

Kind regards,
[Your Name]
```

Rules:

- Mention one real paper or project.
- Ask one specific question.
- Stay under 200 words when possible.

## Template 2: PhD Cover Letter Skeleton

Structure:

1. Opening:
   - exact position title
   - where you saw it
   - why the lab or topic is a fit
2. Master's and fieldwork foundation:
   - one study or thesis
   - one method block
   - one concrete finding or insight
3. Additional projects:
   - two examples
   - what skills they built
4. Fit with the supervisor or lab:
   - one real research contribution from them
   - one question or angle you could contribute to
5. Closing:
   - relocation readiness
   - language readiness
   - concise thank you

Guardrails:

- Do not sound generic or motivational for its own sake.
- Do not list too many bullet points inside the letter body.

## Template 3: Statement Of Purpose Skeleton

Paragraph plan:

1. Hook and overarching research motivation
2. Academic background and master's research
3. Two to three major projects
4. Fit with this programme and supervisor
5. Technical skills and methods
6. Career vision and why this training matters

Rule:

- One paragraph, one purpose.

## Template 4: One-Page Research Idea Note

Sections:

- Title
- Background and rationale
- Objectives and research questions
- Study system and approach
- Expected outcomes and significance

Title rule:

- Use a mechanistic title, not a generic descriptive title.

## Template 5: Application Submission Email

```text
Subject: Application materials for [programme or position title]

Dear [Name / Admissions / Team],

Please find attached my application materials for [programme or position].

Attached:
- CV
- cover letter
- statement of purpose / research note
- any additional required documents

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Kind regards,
[Your Name]
```

## Research Communication Rules

- Mirror the programme's actual wording where truthful.
- Use evidence and fit, not flattery.
- If a publication date, requirement, or supervisor detail is uncertain, write
  `NEEDS_HUMAN_VERIFICATION`.
