# Risk Register

## High-Priority Risks

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation | Early Warning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| No income in first 60 days | High | High | Run parallel outreach and service tracks; keep the free sample offer active; use weekly review to adjust quickly. | No replies after 100 meaningful outreach attempts. |
| PhD rejection or no supervisor | Medium | Medium | Reach out to multiple supervisors, maintain a publishable concept note, and keep the research engine producing artifacts. | No positive response after 10 supervisor emails. |
| Mission drift under revenue pressure | Medium | High | Keep governance constraints immutable and visible in proposals and reviews. | Drafts begin optimizing for money while dropping stewardship language. |
| Autonomous send or workflow bypass | Medium | High | Keep approval gates mandatory and review audit artifacts weekly. | An external message appears without a matching approval trail. |
| Burnout or collapse of daily discipline | High | High | Protect the 06:00 formation slot and include wellbeing signals in reviews. | Repeated skipped formation or weekly review cycles. |
| Data breach or unsafe ecological disclosure | Low | High | Limit sensitive data exposure and keep human approval on high-risk data sharing. | Requests for data beyond the assigned task scope. |
| Connectivity failure | High | Medium | Use local drafting and queue external actions for replay. | Internet checks fail repeatedly or offline queue grows. |

## Response Rules

- Red risks must be named directly in the weekly review.
- Any realized governance breach should create a durable artifact in runtime logs.
- Risk framing should stay practical: what happened, what it threatens, what changes next.
